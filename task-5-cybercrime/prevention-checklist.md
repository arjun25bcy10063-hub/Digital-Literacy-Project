Stay Safe Online: The Essential Checklist
1) PIN is ONLY for Paying: Always remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to receive money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "verify" a credit to your account, it is a scam.

2) Verify the 'Payee' Name: Before hitting 'Pay' on a QR code scan (at a canteen or a photocopy shop), double-check the name that appears on your screen. Scammers sometimes paste their own QR codes over legitimate merchant ones.

3) Activate Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Enable 2FA on your WhatsApp, Instagram, and college email. Even if someone steals your password, they won’t be able to log in without the second code sent to your physical device.

4) Beware of 'Part-Time' Job Scams: If you receive a WhatsApp or Telegram message offering high pay for "liking YouTube videos" or "rating hotels" that eventually asks for a "security deposit," block the number immediately. Legitimate internships never ask you to pay first.

5) Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Open campus or cafe Wi-Fi networks are often unencrypted. Avoid checking your bank balance or logging into student portals while connected to them. Use your mobile data or a trusted VPN instead.

6) The "Urgent" Friend Request: If a friend or professor suddenly messages you from a new account asking for an "urgent" financial favor or an OTP, call them on their known phone number to verify. Scammers frequently impersonate known figures to create a sense of panic.

7) Audit App Permissions: Regularly check which apps have access to your "Contacts," "Gallery," and "Microphone." If a simple calculator app is asking for your contact list, it’s likely harvesting your data.

8) Check the URL Domain: When logging into portals for exams or scholarships, ensure the URL ends in .gov.in or .ac.in. Scammers often use "look-alike" domains (e.g., https://www.google.com/search?q=scholarship-gov-india.com) to phish for your credentials.

If you lose money or your data is compromised, time is of the essence. Reporting within the first two hours (the "Golden Hour") significantly increases the chances of freezing the stolen funds.
