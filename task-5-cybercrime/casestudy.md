Case Study: The Marketplace Trap
Arjun, a student, listed a second-hand engineering textbook on a popular resale platform. Within minutes, a "buyer" contacted him, claiming they wanted to secure the book immediately. The buyer sent a QR code via a messaging app, stating, "I’ve sent the payment. Just scan this and enter your UPI PIN to 'verify' the credit to your account."

The Step-by-Step Execution:

The Hook: The fraudster builds trust by agreeing to the full price without negotiation.

The Deception: They send a "Request Money" link or QR code, masquerading it as a "Receive Money" voucher.

The User Action: Under the impression that a PIN is required to receive funds (it is only required to send), the victim scans the code and enters their secret PIN.

The Theft: The UPI app executes a "Pay" command. The funds are instantly debited from Arjun's account and moved to a "mule" account.

Targets and Consequences
While tech-savvy students and busy professionals are frequent targets, anyone using digital payments is at risk. The consequences include immediate financial loss, compromised banking credentials, and the administrative hurdle of filing a Cyber Cell complaint (via 1930) to attempt a transaction freeze.
