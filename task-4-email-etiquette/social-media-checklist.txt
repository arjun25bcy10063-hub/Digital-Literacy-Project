The Do’s
1) Review your privacy settings regularly: Periodically check who can see your posts and tagged photos to ensure your personal life remains private.
2) Think before you post: Before hitting share, consider if you would be comfortable with a future employer, professor, or family member seeing the content.
3) Build a professional network: Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with alumni, industry professionals, and peers to help bridge the gap between college and your career.
4) Verify information before sharing: Actively fact-check news or viral posts from multiple credible sources to avoid spreading misinformation.
5) Be mindful of your digital footprint: Understand that what you post today can be archived or screenshotted, potentially staying associated with your name for years to come.
The Don’ts
1) Don’t share sensitive personal data: Avoid posting photos of your ID cards, home address, or real-time location, which could compromise your physical or digital safety.
2) Don’t violate academic integrity: Never post or share exam questions, completed assignments, or confidential lecture materials that could lead to university disciplinary action.
3) Don’t engage with "trolls" or cyberbullying: Avoid heated public arguments or disparaging comments; keeping your interactions respectful protects your online reputation.
4) Don’t post content involving illegal activity: Photos or videos showing policy violations or illegal acts can have serious consequences for your enrollment and future background checks.
5) Don’t vent about professors or employers: Keep frustrations about coursework or workplaces offline; public complaints can appear unprofessional and may damage important relationships.
